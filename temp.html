Extracting structural features irrelevant of the sequence, just from the sequence.

ESM2 assigns each residue a 1280-dimensional embedding vector — not just encoding what the amino acid is, but where it sits biochemically: its charge environment, hydrophobicity context, likely contacts, and functional role.

This is possible because the transformer's attention mechanism lets every residue attend to every other residue in the chain. By training on 250 million evolutionary sequences, the model learns that certain residue patterns co-occur because they are in structural contact — not because they are sequence-adjacent.

The result: two catalytic histidines in completely different scaffolds produce similar embedding vectors, because ESM2 has seen both in the same functional context across evolution.

Each vector encodes the residue's full neighboring biochemical environment — the chemistry, geometry, and functional role of its structural context — derived entirely from the 1D sequence.
